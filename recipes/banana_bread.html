Banana Bread

Image Credit: Kelly Hamilton

Description

If you're anything like me, then you probably hate spotty, overripe bananas.
You also probably feel pretty bad about having to throw them out when you
fail to eat them before they get all mushy. Thankfully, banana bread is pretty
easy to make and only requires basic baking ingredients that most people
probably already have sitting in the pantry. Go ahead and use up those spotty
bananas!

Ingredients

  • 1 2/3 cups of all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 1/2 tsp baking soda
  • 1/2 cup butter, unsalted
  • 1/2 cup sugar
  • 1/2 tsp baking powder
  • 2 eggs
  • 1 tbsp neutral-flavored oil (eg, canola)
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • 4 bananas

How to Make

  1. Bring butter and eggs to room temperature. Pre-heat the oven to 350 degress Farenheit (175 degrees Celcius).
  2. Put flour, salt, baking soda, and baking powder into a bowl and whisk together. Cream sugar and butter together in a separate bowl.
  3. Add eggs, oil, and vanilla to sugar mixture and beat until well-blended.
  4. Mash bananas with fork and add to egg mixture.
  5. Mix dry and wet ingredients together, then pour batter into a small loaf pan.
  6. Bake in the oven for about an hour. A toothpick poked into the middle should come out clean. Enjoy!
