Southern Biscuits

Description

I've always preferred biscuits to English muffins, probably because of how buttery and flakey they are.
They can be eaten as a side with pretty much any meal and taste great with sausage gravy, jam, or even
just plain and fresh out of the oven!

Ingredients

  • 2 cups self-rising flour
  • 1 tsp sugar
  • 1/4 cup butter, lard, or shortening
  • 2/3 cup milk or buttermilk

How to Make

  1. Pre-heat the oven to 450 F (232 C). Whisk dry ingredients together in a bowl, then cut the butter in
    with a pastry cutter. Dough should resemble crums about the size of peas.
  2. Gradually add milk and mix until dough is just wet.
  3. Gently roll dough into a ball and press down on lightly floured surface. Fold the dough in half, then
    press again. Repeat this process three times, then roll out dough into about 1/2 thickness and cut biscuits.
  4. Arrange biscuits on a baking sheet and bake for about 10-14 minutes or until lightly browned.
